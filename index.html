Finishing Strong · Steve Farrar

On running the whole race

So many begin the race well — full of fire, full of promise — and then, one by one, they fall away before the finish. What marks a life is not how brightly it started, but whether it is still standing, still faithful, still walking with God when the finish line finally comes into view. The few who finish strong are not the most gifted; they are the ones who kept showing up, kept choosing faithfulness in the small, unseen hours, long after applause had gone quiet.

The book Victoria has been urged to finish reading — the paragraph that stayed.

The Pilgrim's Progress · John Bunyan, 1684

For a fearless woman

Then said he, I am going to my Father's; and though with great difficulty I have got hither, yet now I do not repent me of all the trouble I have been at to arrive where I am. My sword I give to him that shall succeed me in my pilgrimage, and my courage and skill to him that can get it. My marks and scars I carry with me, to be a witness for me that I have fought His battles who will now be my rewarder. So he passed over, and all the trumpets sounded for him on the other side.

Fearless. Valiant-for-truth. Victoria, all the way through.

Where the story actually starts

Before any of these messages, before the timestamps and the devotionals, there was 2018 — the year this friendship began. The chat picks up two years in, already deep, already sure of itself. Everything below is a continuation, not a beginning.

October 2020sad · tender

Steady, in a hard season

By the time the messages pick up, this friendship was already two years deep — steady enough to hold a frightening season without flinching. A hard year, friends lost, uncertainty everywhere, and still this:

"May God be with you, do not allow the situation weigh you down, or make you dismay. God has promised, He's able to perform. Let's hold on to heaven's promises and shut out the dismal information."22 Oct 2020, 15:04

Victoria's reply was one word, but it was everything: "AMEN." That single word became the rhythm of the years that followed.

24 October 2020happy

"There's only one Victoria"

A message that has never once been taken back:

"There's only one Victoria, that's my Victoria... I'm lucky to have her as a friend. May God keep her for her generation. She's a fearless person. We've fought too, very well. I don't fear her."24 Oct 2020, 00:05

Fearless. That word would follow her through everything that came next.

24 September 2021misunderstanding

The charger, and the "number one stressor"

A small mix-up over a phone charger — wrong cable, wrong colour, a joke about crying over it — tipped into something more honest. Victoria, only half-teasing, told him exactly what she thought.

"You're the number one stressor in my life...."Victoria, 24 Sept 2021

Isaac didn't laugh it off. He sat with it, and answered with a rare kind of honesty:

"When I look back to that faithful day we first talked up till now, it's obvious that I'm really a huge stress contributor... Please, I'm sorry. Aside that, that's why most times I don't want to get involved, I fear that I'll cause you nothing but trouble. But abba, God is on the throne... if he wills, the talk we had that day will soon be history, and if he wills, it will be historic."24 Sept 2021, 21:19

By the next line she was calling him "old man" again. Some misunderstandings are just love, learning to be more careful with itself.

14 September 2023sad · tender

Sung to, over the phone, while sick

Some memories only surface when you're reminiscing out loud. This one came up on an ordinary Thursday night — Victoria remembering a season she'd almost forgotten she survived.

"When I think of your IT, I remember you singing hymns and praying for me over the phone while I was sick. Kai! You spent airtime."14 Sept 2023, 22:15

She was quick to redirect the credit, the way she always does: "Whatever you think I've done, know that it's Jesus who did it." But the hymns were real, and so was the phone bill.

25 February 2025misunderstanding

The apology for a vague answer

She had called the day before, worried, asking about something important. The answer she got back was thin — and Isaac knew it the moment he sent it.

"I owe you an apology. Yesterday, you made an effort to call me to ask about very important happenings. I do not take that for granted. However, I gave very vague responses. You didn't deserve that, and I shouldn't have done so."25 Feb 2025, 12:06 pm

No excuse offered, just an honest one: "What warranted my actions, nothing but selfishness maybe, an inability to own up, and an unnecessary fear." By 3:30 that afternoon she was already asking to talk again — the kind of friendship where an honest apology closes the gap fast.

20 March 2025happy · growth

Convocation, done and dusted

Between a lost phone, a brother's hospital scare, and certificate collection stress, convocation finally arrived.

"We anticipate more sha... I understand that you have been very overwhelmed with many things, but thank God, it has been victory after another. Your convocation, collection of cert, found your phone, your brother's health — and so many other matters."20 Mar 2025, 3:45 pm

"How's the French?" to "You will be corresponding with Francophone countries"

In their very first week of talking, Isaac was already asking about her French — a small, ordinary question. Five years of "oui," "merci beaucoup," and "je suis ici" later, that ordinary question became a calling.

"We need a french interpreter for our mancom meeting tonight... don't worry, be like that digital work you're doing with Zicounsels is too small. Na Francophone countries you will be corresponding with."23 Aug 2025, 16:30

Watching someone's small, patient habit become their calling — that's a particular kind of joy only a friend of many years gets to witness.

Her aunt's accident

He noticed her absence from church before she said a word about why.

18 July 2026 — this year's letter

Happy birthday, Oluwaronke Victoria.

Twenty-four. And I want you to know this letter isn't the first time I've tried to say this — just the first time I've put it all in one place. I went back through years of messages to find the moments I meant the most, so you'd know this isn't something an app wrote for you. It's mine.

Back in January 2022, I told you:

"Thank you for the Amazing Friendship and companionship through my university days, now I know I had the best."6 Jan 2022, 20:09

That Christmas, I tried again, longer, because one line was never going to be enough:

"You've been a huge blessing to me, I thank God for bringing our paths to smash each other."25 Dec 2022, 21:48

And more recently, on an ordinary Thursday morning, I said it plainer than I ever had:

"I am not your God or your lover, but you mean so much to me, and I do cherish you and the friendship."20 Mar 2025, 09:31

All of that is still true today, maybe truer. You've forgiven fast, been forgiven fast, learned a language nobody made you learn, stayed valiant through things you never asked to be praised for, and been the friend who prays first and explains later. No vex — but I don't think you know how rare that is.

So may God keep you for your generation. May He honour every quiet, unseen faithfulness the way He always has. And may this year be one where you feel — the way you did over that cake — unmistakably, thoroughly loved.

With gratitude that keeps growing,
Isaac

P.S. — Somebody still owes you corn from Lusada. Roasted, not boiled.
